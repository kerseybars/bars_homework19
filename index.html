Hey there, friend! I'm Kersey and a junior UI/UX designer based in Eugene, Oregon (Go Ducks!).

I began my adult life with the lofty ambition, which I accomplished, of being the first person in my family to graduate from college. I graduated in 2015 with a bachelor's degree from Willamette University with coursework in Exercise and Health Science.

After finishing my undergraduate degree, I entered the construction field and worked as a Precision Tune Up Technician for an HVAC company and a Journeyman Painter. So, I'm comfortable with being uncomfortable. For the past 7 years, I have been working as the often the only woman in a group full of men, so I've learned how to effectively communicate with a multitude of people from a variety of backgrounds.

When I'm not working, I enjoy training for, and competing in triathlons, reading books, crafting, and spending time with my two cats.

Because of my construction background, I excel at problem solving and “seeing how things fit”. I believe that with the advances in technology and society moving towards a more inclusive space, that all people should be able to enjoy browsing websites. I imagine my designs with a "barely there" mentality; I don't want people to have to think when they're using what I've created. As such, I strive to create intuitive, accessible, and fun designs that can be used and appreciated by all.

My Travel Buddy

While conducting user research, a common piece of information was that the experience people had while traveling was significantly more important than the destination. As such, I designed an app that would help users find a personalized experince within their travel destination.

This was a 4-week long solo project, where I used Miro, Figma, Invision, and Google Suite to develop a a travel application that allowed my users to cater their experiences based off their budget, number of people, location, and time frame.
